WHAT'S YOUR STORY?

2015 — 2019

Majored in computer science & engineering at MIT, specializing in Human-Computer Interaction. Graduated with honours, Tau Beta Pi and Eta Kappa Nu. Humanities concentration in Theater Arts, with a focus on the intersection of sports and performance studies.

Studied at ETH Zurich in Switzerland on exchange and taught STEM in Germany, Ghana, and Hong Kong. Researched at the MIT Media Lab and CSAIL. Interned at startups, Priceline.com, and Microsoft. Blogged for MIT Admissions and served as president of a fraternity.

Continued competing in the Junior Grand Prix series for two more seasons. Another US Junior National medal and the 2017 and 2018 Collegiate National Championship titles.

Retired from the sport and circumnavigated around the world in 90 days, journeying across five continents before moving to Seattle.

2008 — 2015

Continued training full-time while attending grade school. Painful ankle injury in 2012 sidelined the next competitive season and nearly ended skating career. Worked part time at a law firm and studied some violin, Chinese, and Japanese.

Two national medals in the Novice and Junior divisions and a spot on Team USA in the junior ranks. International assignments in the Netherlands and Japan.

Graduated Piedmont High School as valedictorian and committed to attend MIT.

1997 — 2008

Born and raised in the San Francisco Bay Area. Kept busy with Kumon, tap-dancing, Chinese brush painting, and choir.

Took my first steps on the ice at age six alongside my older brother. Won the 2008 US Juvenile National title.
